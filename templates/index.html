GOAL

Finding the right place to invest in rental properties or the right ways to improve current conditions is no longer gambling.

Provide investors and local communities with separate optimized improvement schemes for the rental market integrating their local social and physical conditions and budgets.

WHY

Socio-spatial features, including location, demographics, social environments, local transportation, and characteristics of neighborhood-built environments, could be determinant factors of rental prices besides rental properties themselves (for example, the numbers of bedrooms and bathrooms, furnishing conditions, views, and building/community amenities). Particularly in dense areas/cities like Manhattan, commute services and city life features affect people’s housing choices with a low car use compared to suburban areas.

HOW

  • Predict average neighborhood rental prices based on the neighborhood socio-spatial features.
  • Identify the key environmental features for increasing the neighborhood rental market values.

Where did the data come from?

- American Community Survey (ACS) 5-Year Data (2009-2018)

- AARP.org Livability Index

- Census Mapping Files: TIGER/Line Shapefiles

The project recursively retrieved the data from the livability index map for the targeted States and associated the neighborhood information with internal points of census data to combine the housing and spatial information.

Models and Results

The project extracted 43 detailed features from six categories (excluded housing cost itself) indcluding demographics to explore the individual effects.

  • The results from three methods (random forest, Ada boosting, and gradient boosting) showed the 43 features provided 80%-84% accuracies (calcuated from MAPE) to predict neighborhood average rents."
  • The top important features the models observed were: transportation (number of jobs accessible within a 45-minute transit commute and number of jobs accessible within a 45-minute automobile commute), street safety design (annual average number of fatal crashes per 100,000 people), accessibility to markets (number of grocery stores and farmers’ markets within a half-mile), walkable environments (estimated walk trips per household per day), and mixed land use (mix of jobs within a mile).
